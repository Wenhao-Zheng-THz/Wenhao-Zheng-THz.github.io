Research

Our research focuses on ultrafast terahertz (THz) spectroscopy—especially on-chip integrated THz platforms— to reveal low-energy electrodynamics, correlations, and nonequilibrium transport in low-dimensional quantum materials.

On-chip Terahertz Spectroscopy

Integrated THz sources/detectors and calibrated pipelines for device-level spectroscopy.

  • On-chip THz emitters/detectors and compact spectrometer architectures
  • System response calibration & quantitative extraction (E(t), σ(ω), ε(ω))
  • Compatibility with cryogenic and magneto-transport environments
On-chip THz THz-TDS Calibration

Correlated Electrodynamics & Superconductivity

THz complex conductivity fingerprints of low-energy excitations and collective dynamics.

  • Complex conductivity σ(ω) across phase transitions and correlated regimes
  • Low-energy modes, scattering, and spectral weight transfer
  • Field/temperature tuning with cryogenic THz spectroscopy
Complex σ(ω) Superconductivity Correlations

Ultrafast Carrier & Heat Transport

Multi-timescale dynamics from femtoseconds to nanoseconds in low-dimensional materials.

  • Carrier relaxation, hot-carrier transport, and transient conductivity
  • Lattice heating & thermal dissipation pathways
  • Coupling between photophysics and electrical transport
Ultrafast Transport Thermal

Quantum Transport in 2D Devices

Gate-tunable transport and intrinsic electrodynamics in quantum materials and heterostructures.

  • Low-temperature quantum transport (gating, magneto-transport, Hall)
  • 2D materials and vdW heterostructures: devices and characterization
  • Connecting DC transport with THz electrodynamics
2D Devices Cryogenic Quantum Transport
