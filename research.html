Research

Our research focuses on ultrafast terahertz (THz) spectroscopy—especially on-chip integrated THz platforms— to reveal low-energy electrodynamics, correlations, and nonequilibrium transport in low-dimensional quantum materials.
Two directions, one shared motivation.
We build robust THz measurement capabilities and use them to understand quantum materials and devices, bridging time-domain signals with quantitative electrodynamic response and transport physics.
Direction 1

超快泵浦太赫兹光谱：非平衡载流子动力学

Ultrafast pump–THz probes for disentangling nonequilibrium charge dynamics across wide time scales.

  • Transient photoconductivity and carrier scattering/relaxation following photoexcitation
  • Hot-carrier and energy-flow pathways (carrier–phonon coupling, thermalization, dissipation)
  • Multi-timescale dynamics from femtoseconds to nanoseconds, connected to transport behavior
Pump–THz Nonequilibrium Carrier Dynamics Thermal Transport
Direction 2

低温输运与片上集成太赫兹光谱：量子材料关联电子态（如超导等）

Combining cryogenic transport and on-chip THz spectroscopy to probe correlated electronic states and low-energy electrodynamics.

  • Complex conductivity σ(ω) as a fingerprint of low-energy excitations and collective dynamics
  • Field/temperature tuning with cryogenic and magneto-transport compatible THz measurements
  • On-chip integrated THz platforms for device-level spectroscopy and quantitative extraction (E(t), σ(ω), ε(ω))
